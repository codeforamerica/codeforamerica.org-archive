Program Overview

By and large, it is companies that create the technology infrastructure—software, tools, and hardware—that governments use to do their internal work or to deliver services to citizens. These companies play a critical role in making government work for the people, by the people, in the 21st century.

The goal of the Companies Program is to identify the skills and business practices that are needed for the vendor ecosystem to work in the interests of the American people, and support the companies who practice them. Our aim is to expand opportunity for companies of all sizes whose participation in the government technology ecosystem furthers the CfA vision.

We work with mission-aligned startups to improve their access to the govtech marketplace; with established firms to adapt to the changes taking place in this market; with local governments to aid them in acquiring and implementing new technologies effectively; and all of the above to facilitate new opportunities and change the rules of the game.

Accelerator

The Code for America Accelerator “turbo-charges” select govtech companies by providing them an opportunity to amplify market awareness of their product, to access a wealth of business training and advice, and to be introduced to a broad network of potential investors and government leaders. The program runs from July to October and those chosen receive $25K in funding, mentorship from celebrated govtech veterans and access to the Code for America network.

  • Learn more

Incubator

The CfA Incubator is a no-nonsense, highly focused experience helping teams build a sustainable business from their current product ideas. The program runs from December to June to support the sustainability of applications emerging from the fellowship, to develop apps into scalable business enterprises, and to retain these talented govtech technologists where they are most needed: strengthening the ecosystem.

  • Learn more

Mentors

The mentors in the companies program are leaders in the technology and public administration space on a variety of topics, such as customer research, entrepreneurship, finance, legal, marketing, product, sales, strategy, UX and venture capital.
