Our Approach

The economic development focus area works to improve economic opportunity for all people and their communities. Economic outcomes depend on a place-based context of communities, and range across concentrated growth, revitalization or advancement of core governmental services and benefits necessary for business and job growth.

Code for America works across a wide variety of teams who contribute to these outcomes—including municipal economic development departments, public-private economic development corporations, redevelopment authorities, and non-profit organizations. Together, we develop digital services that measurably improve economic opportunity, and help these teams retool their ways of delivering services to be more effective in the digital world.

How we work

Code for America’s focus areas bring together work across all of our programs, delivering an impact greater than what each program might achieve on its own. Across our programs, we undertake deep research into the delivery of critical services, develop new digital approaches to economic opportunity, incubate digital products as they mature, and work with the highest levels of government support to scale digital services. Throughout these efforts, we work to empower our partner organizations through the Principles of 21st Century Government.

Original Research and Digital Work

Fellowship teams work with cities and counties to develop innovative digital approaches to delivering key government services.

Building and scaling Digital Services

As fellowship teams move on, we work to transform promising new technologies into the mature digital services citizens deserve in the 21st century.

Helping Governments Deliver Digital Services

We document what we learn to make building digital government easy to understand and copy through our library and Peer Network.

Partnering with governments to produce original digital services

The Code for America Fellowship Program sends teams of technologists into local governments across the country to work full-time for a year in partnership with government officials and the community. The fellows apply 21st Century Government Principles to important problems, focused on health, economic development, and safety and justice. While the product of the Fellowship is typically an early stage application that improves the delivery of a government service, the process acts as a vehicle for driving cultural and structural change inside of government.

Impacting Small Business and Workforce Development

The economic development focus area targets interventions that impact small businesses and low income populations to:

  • Involve a broader community of local businesses in growing innovation and tech-based economies
  • Reduce barriers to low-income families and entrepreneurs in taking advantage of government programs and services
  • Broaden access to skills-building and upward mobility of the workforce

Ongoing Work

We have worked with more than 50 communities to support growth and healthy economic development practice within three strategic areas:

Innovation

Goal: Expanding competitiveness for future economic growth

Strategy: Open data legislation, offices of innovation, data-driven services

Apps: Transitmit, Streetmix, Text My Bus

Revitalization

Goal: Improving economic viability of shrinking cities

Strategy: Blight mitigation, downtown redevelopment, asset mapping

Apps: Civic Insight, LocalData

Advancement

Goal: Advancing and streamlining existing service delivery

Strategy: Small business development, workforce development, permits and zoning applications

Apps: Development FastPass, OpenCounter, Primer Peso, Biz Friendly
