Our Approach

Code for America encourages new strategies, including cross-agency collaboration among law enforcement, health and human services departments, schools systems, and others, to implement evidence-based strategies to keep individuals from cycling in and out of the justice system. With particular attention to those communities most impacted by crime and violence, our work is focused on increasing public safety, strengthening public trust, and saving governments money.

This is an emerging area of Code for America’s work. In the past few years, Code for America has worked with Atlanta and Louisville on key issues in their court and jail systems. In Atlanta, we helped improve court efficiency and, via text messaging updates, helped get busy citizens to their court date and avoid jail. In Louisville, Code for America built a real-time dashboard so prosecutors, administrators, and police officers could see the population numbers in the jail and other vital statistics to better inform release, sentencing, and placement decisions.

As part of the 2015 Fellowship, Code for America is partnering with Indianapolis and Vallejo on better integrating data into public safety efforts and strengthening law enforcement relations with the community.

Areas of Interest

The safety and justice focus area targets interventions that impact the justice system’s capacity to deliver fair and effective outcomes with goals that include:

  1. Making criminal justice data, and other relevant data, better integrated, accessible, and accurate to improve decision making at the individual and system-wide level.
  2. Facilitating early connections to appropriate treatment and services for individuals in the justice system to improve public safety outcomes and save money.
  3. Improving community safety and access to support for those individuals and communities most likely to be victimized by crime.

How we work

Code for America’s focus areas bring together work across all of our programs, delivering an impact greater than what each program might achieve on its own. Across our programs, we undertake deep research into the delivery of critical services, develop new digital approaches to safety and justice, incubate digital products as they mature, and work with the highest levels of government support to scale digital services. Throughout these efforts, we work to empower our partner organizations through the Principles of 21st Century Government.

Original Research and Digital Work

Fellowship teams work with cities and counties to develop innovative digital approaches to delivering key government services.

Building and scaling Digital Services

As fellowship teams move on, we work to transform promising new technologies into the mature digital services citizens deserve in the 21st century.

Helping Governments Deliver Digital Services

We document what we learn to make building digital government easy to understand and copy through our library and Peer Network.

Partnering with governments to produce original digital services

The Code for America Fellowship Program sends teams of technologists into local governments across the country to work full-time for a year in partnership with government officials and the community. The fellows apply 21st Century Government Principles to important problems, focused on health, economic development, and safety and justice. While the product of the Fellowship is typically an early stage application that improves the delivery of a government service, the process acts as a vehicle for driving cultural and structural change inside of government.

Our Projects

  • Courtbot

    Courtbot is a simple web service for handling court case data. It enables city residents who receive traffic tickets to text their citation number to get information on how to pay the ticket or get information and reminders about their court dates.

    Learn more

  • Louisville Jail Dashboard

    Jail Population Management Dashboard helps judges, district attorneys and other stakeholders understand the conditions in the metro jail and use this data to visualize how their decisions affect program, facility and inmate outcomes.

    Learn more

  • AddressIQ

    AddressIQ is a web application that analyzes city data to help identify addresses with a high number of 911 calls and support finding more cost-effective ways to provide those individuals who are high utilizers of emergency services with care and resources.

    Learn more

  • Criminal Case Search

    Criminal Case Search, a web application that enables staff to run customized real-time searches of criminal case information such as defendant demographics, criminal history, criminal charge, and court scheduling information to determine eligibility for a range of alternatives to incarceration.

    Learn more
