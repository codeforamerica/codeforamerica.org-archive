About the Fellowship

The Fellowship program sends teams of technologists into local governments across the country to work full time for a year in partnership with government officials. The Fellows apply 21st Century Government Principles to important problems, focused on health, economic development and safety & justice.

What do the fellows do?

Over the course of the project period, the Fellows immerse themselves in the community, researching user needs, meeting with stakeholders, proposing solutions and collaborating with the government to build technology using an iterative, user-centered and data-driven approach.

While the product of the Fellowship is a typically a early stage application that improves the delivery of a government service or function, the process acts as a vehicle for driving cultural and structural change inside of governmentâ€”encouraging innovation, improving tolerance for risk, and increasing the organizations capacity for transparency and engagement.

How are cities selected?

Each year CfA selects 8-10 local government partners from across the country and 24-30 fellows for the Program through a competitive application process.

Interested in hearing more about the selection process? Reach out to us to discuss the fellowship program and how to enlist the fellows to help your government.
