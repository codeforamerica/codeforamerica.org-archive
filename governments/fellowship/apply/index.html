City Fellowship Application

City Application for 2016 Open January 15, 2015

The below application is a sample from the Code for America’s 2015 fellowship program – an 11-month program where a team of developers, researchers and designers work alongside your staff to solve local government problems.

If you're interested in applying for 2016, please contact Luke Norris, Director of Government Relations, to learn more.

Prepping Your Application

There are a number of things you can do to build a successful application and become a Code for America fellowship city. Check out the fellowship FAQ, review the program timing and check out the alumni city outcomes.

Previous 2015 City Fellowship Application

Below are sample questions that you'll be asked in the application:

  1. Your title & organization
    e.g. Deputy Mayor, City of Charlotte, NC
  2. Which Government are you applying for?
    e.g. San Mateo County, CA or Burlington, VT
  3. Are you an employee of that Government?
    Are you the primary contact?
  4. Town/City/County Website
    Do you have the support of your local Government to apply for the CfA Fellowship Program?
  5. What are your goals for participating in the Fellowship?
    Expand on your desired goals & outcomes. Be specific. (300 word maximum)
  6. In addition to building a custom app/apps for your local government, what existing CfA apps would you most like to redeploy? (200 words maximum)
    eg. RecordTrac, Adopt-A, etc.
  7. Strong sponsorship is key to the programs success. What local government leaders will take an active roll to support this program? (300 words maximum)
    Have you secured a budget for this project?
  8. If yes, what percentage has been secured?
    How confident are you in securing matching funds from local businesses and community partners?
  9. What else should we consider about your local government that makes you an excellent candidate? (300 words maximum)
    eg. Recent policies, tech-savvy Mayor's office, fiber all around town, strong pre-existing tech community, etc.
  10. What steps have you taken to promote innovation in Government? (300 words maximum)
    eg. Innovation Office, Open Data Portal, UX Workshops, etc.
  11. How did you hear about the Code for America Fellowship Program?
    What other Code for America programs are you interested in?
