Denver

Denver is the largest city and the capital of Colorado. It is the home to tech companies like Pivotal Labs, SendGrid and McAfee, as well as early-stage incubators like Galvanize. In addition to the 2014 Fellows working in Denver to improve a variety of city services, Denver’s Code for America Brigade brings together hundreds of members of the community to engage in regular hackathons and discussions.

the 2014 Fellows Colorado Code for Communities Denver Open Datasets

The City of Denver has enlisted the 2014 Code for America fellows to work alongside city staff and shape government services.

The 2014 Fellowship in Denver

The Code for America Denver is working with the City and County of Denver to demonstrate innovation with open data and good service design. Rather than focusing on one large project, the 2014 Fellowship team in Denver has decided to tackle several smaller problems the city and its’ residents face everyday. They've created Cute Pets Denver, an API and Twitter bot that tweets out adorable pictures of adoptable pets from Denver’s animal shelter to help save these animals from euthanization. This project has already been re-deployed in cities like Boston, Austin, and Norfolk.

The team has also built StreetSweep.co a website that allows residents to sign up for street sweeping reminders near their home so they avoid tickets.

Finally, the Fellows are building a dashboard of metrics from the marijuana industry to help the city lend its voice to a national conversation about marijuana legislation.
