Managing Public Records Requests

This event will air on March 20, 2014 at 10am PT.

Guest Speakers

  • John Kaehny

    Executive Director, Reinvent San Francisco

    John Kaehny, executive director and founding board member, and is the co-chair of the New York City Transparency Working Group. In that role he helped create New York City’s Open Data Law. John has been immersed in New York freedom of information, open government and public participation issues for decades.

    More

  • Richa Agarwal

    2013 Code for America Fellow and co-founder, PostCode.io

    Richa is a software developer and lover of technology, civic engagement, and the outdoors. She was a 2013 Code for America Fellow on the Oakland team, where she helped build RecordTrac, an app for streamlining public records requests. She is now a founder of civic startup PostCode.io.

    More

  • Karen Boyd

    Communications Director, City of Oakland

    Karen Boyd is Communications Director for the City of Oakland, CA. She was a city partner during the 2013 Code for America Fellowship where she collaborated with the team on the RecordTrac project.

    More

About

March 16-22 is Sunshine Week! In honor of the occasion, we'll be taking a deep dive into the public records request process to examine the ins and outs of effectively tracking, routing, and responding to citizen requests for public information. John Kaehny will be talking about the work of Reinvent Albany and Karen Boyd will share a case study of how the City of Oakland has evolved their approach to open public records; why open data can save your organization time and money when responding to records request; and how technology can be used to help streamline the process for citizens and city employees alike.

This event is hosted by the Code for America Peer Network, CfA's community of innovators working in local government from over thirty cities and counties. Learn more about the Peer Network and how you can get involved here.
