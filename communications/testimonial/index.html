Peer Network

"One of the big things about Code for America is that it gives people whose motivation is to serve the public, a reminder of why they're here and a chance to talk to other people. You couldn't do Code for America if every city was its own lab. The fact that there's a shared training group, meetings where people talk to each other, cross talk, and that ability to meet others who are excited about the same things you're excited about -- that's a big part of what powers Code for America" - Clay Shirky, Author

“Open Data is critical to any strong government. The data belongs to the people. It needs to be open the from top level data all the way down into reports -- not just for the people who already know what's going on, but also for entrepreneurs to create opportunities and offer information and knowledge to the public. We have a standing executive order for all data to be open.” - Mayor Greg Fisher, City of Louisville (2013 Fellowship City)

"Peer Network members benefit from the efforts taking place across the country to make open data more useful and more powerful." - Mark Headd, Chief Data Officer, City of Philadelphia(2011 and 2012 Fellowship City)
